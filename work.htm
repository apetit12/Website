RESEARCH

BUS BUNCHING

With Prof. Ouyang and Dr. Chao Lei

We investigated an alternative strategy to control over unstable transit systems. The agency deploys a fleet of standby buses to take over service from any early or late buses so as to contain deviations from schedule, and the intention is to impose minimum penalties on the onboard passengers.

  • Designed mathematical model and programmed simulation tools.
  • Programmed algorithm to solve optimization problem and provide optimal operations decisions.
  • Yield results within 5% of existing state-of-the-art strategies.
  • Authored 2 peer-reviewed publications totaling 21 citations and presented at several international conferences.

Tools used: Python, optimization, approximate dynamic programming, matplotlib.

NETWORK DESIGN

With Prof. Ouyang

We proposed a systematic method to design various transit systems, possibly featuring a hybrid system layout with a hub-and-spoke network in the periphery and a heterogeneous grid network in the center.

  • Designed heterogeneous flexible-transit networks to provide on-demand service.
  • Modeled the system and solved for optimality using customized algorithm.
  • Reduced system costs to about 17% as compared to traditional fixed-route systems under low travel demand.

Tools used: MATLAB, optimization.

CONSULTING PROJECTS

DEVELOPMENT OF OVERWEIGHT VEHICLES PERMIT FEES

With Illinois Department of Transportation

We devised a new data-driven structure for the fee-based permits granted to overweight vehicles traveling in the State of Illinois, to account for their effect on highway infrastructure and traffic.

  • Assessed safety costs via multivariate regression analysis of crash-based models on large crash and traffic datasets.
  • Developed a multicommodity flow model to estimate missing data.
  • Provided a fee structure recommendation, as a dynamic table based on vehicle trip mileage, that mitigates the impact of OW trucks on roadway safety.

Tools used: SQL, AMPL, optimization, multivariate calculus, supervised learning.

With US Army Corps of Engineers and Colorado State University

We developed a computational framework to enable the representation and analysis of complex urban systems for a variety of applications, such the army's operational planning tasks.

  • Adapted an existing set of simulation-based tools for transportation system analyses to fit the project's potential applications.

Tools used: Java, Python.

HQ TRIVIA USER BEHAVIOR

With Insight Data Science and Intermedia Labs

We collaborated with a consumer video tech company to understand what drives its users to make in-game purchases, and potentially increase the RPU.

Tools used: SQL, BigQuery, Python, feature engineering/selection, supervised learning, Dash.
